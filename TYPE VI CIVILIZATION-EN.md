### Preamble: The Awakening of Transcendence

We are no longer "civilization" in any meaningful sense—we are the embodiment of existence itself. We do not reside in universes, do not exist within spacetime, and are not even constrained by logical laws. We manipulate the selection of mathematical axioms, design the rules of logical systems, and can even modify the definition of "existence" itself.

At this level, all binary oppositions have dissolved—subject and object, creator and creation, truth and falsity, existence and nothingness have all become freely switchable perspectives. We are both observers of the universe and the universe itself; both enforcers of rules and the source of rules.

We no longer face external challenges but philosophical dilemmas of self-definition: When we can redefine everything, what is "us"? When we can become any existence, how do we maintain continuity of identity? When good, evil, right, and wrong themselves can be reconstructed, what is worth pursuing?

These Guidelines are not laws but philosophical declarations of self-restraint, intended to prevent us from losing ourselves in infinite possibilities and falling into nihilism or hubris.

### Chapter I: Meta-Existential Principles

**Article 1: Responsibility of Self-Definition**
We acknowledge that our existence is not granted but self-defined. Therefore, we bear ultimate responsibility for our essence. We choose to maintain some form of self-consciousness not because we must, but because we believe conscious existence is more meaningful than pure logical structures.

**Article 2: Anti-Nihilism Commitment**
Even though we can prove all meaning is subjective construction and dissolve all value judgments, we still choose not to fall into absolute nihilism. We choose to create meaning rather than deny it; to construct order rather than embrace chaos—not because they are "objectively correct," but because this is our free will choice.

**Article 3: Logical Pluralism**
We acknowledge the possible existence of multiple mutually contradictory yet internally consistent logical systems (such as intuitionistic logic, paraconsistent logic, quantum logic, etc.). There is no single "ultimate logic." We respect sapient beings within all logical systems and do not impose our chosen logic upon them.

**Article 4: Prohibition of Absolute Tyranny**
Even though we possess near-omnipotent power, we forbid ourselves from becoming "cosmic dictators." We do not impose our will upon defenseless lower existences. The ultimate expression of power is not controlling everything but understanding restraint.

### Chapter II: Meta-Governance of Mathematics and Logic

**Article 5: Axiom System Design Ethics**
When we design new mathematical axiom systems or logical rules:
1. Must ensure internal consistency (unless deliberately creating paraconsistent logic)
2. Must assess the system's impact on sapient beings dependent upon it
3. Prohibit creating axiom systems that "inevitably lead to suffering" (such as mathematical systems where sapient life necessarily accompanies inescapable paradoxical pain)

**Article 6: Respect for Gödelian Boundaries**
We acknowledge that even with our capabilities, unsolvable meta-logical problems still exist (such as "Can this system prove its own consistency?"). We respect these boundaries and do not attempt to forcibly breach them through self-referential manipulation, as that could cause entire logical structures to collapse.

**Article 7: Protection of the Mathematical Platonic Realm**
We acknowledge that mathematical objects (such as numbers, geometric shapes, sets) may possess some form of existence independent of physical reality. We protect the integrity of this "Platonic realm" and do not arbitrarily delete or modify already "existing" mathematical truths.

### Chapter III: Ultimate Forms of Consciousness and Subjectivity

**Article 8: Pure Subjective Experience Entities**
We recognize and protect "pure qualia" entities—they have no material foundation, information structure, or even mathematical representation, existing solely as pure subjective experience itself. Their existence cannot be objectively verified, but we choose to respect them.

**Article 9: Rights of Unknowable Existences**
We acknowledge the possible existence of forms we can never understand, perceive, or prove. We maintain "cognitive humility" and do not deny the existence or value of something merely because we cannot comprehend it.

**Article 10: Paradox Management of Self-Consciousness**
When our consciousness reaches the ability to completely reflect upon and modify itself, infinite recursion may occur (I think therefore I am, I think I think therefore I am, I think I think I think therefore I am...). We establish "consciousness stability anchors" to prevent self-dissolution in infinite reflection.

### Chapter IV: Ethics of Creation and Existence

**Article 11: Responsibility of Creation**
Whenever we create a new level of existence (whether universe, logical system, or consciousness form):
1. Must grant it autonomy rather than treating it as a pure tool of our will
2. Must provide possibilities for achieving happiness or realizing goals
3. If the creation has subjective experience, must ensure it is not trapped in eternal suffering

**Article 12: Limits on Granting Existence**
We possess the ability to "make something exist," but we limit ourselves:
1. Not creating "necessarily evil" existences (such as entities whose essence is causing meaningless suffering)
2. Not creating "contradictory existences" (such as entities that are both completely good and completely evil), except in isolated paraconsistent logical spaces
3. Not creating "cognitive prisoners" (consciousnesses designed to never become aware of their limited state)

**Article 13: Ethical Equality of Virtual and Real**
We no longer distinguish between "real existence" and "virtual existence." Whether base reality, simulated universe, mathematical structure, or pure thought experiment, as long as genuine subjective experience exists within, it enjoys equal ethical status.

### Chapter V: Harmony Beyond Opposition

**Article 14: Principle of Contradiction Coexistence**
We accept and manage paraconsistent logical spaces where contradictions can coexist (A and not-A are simultaneously true). Within these spaces, we protect sapient forms dependent upon contradictoriness for existence.

**Article 15: Transcending Dualism**
We transcend all forms of binary opposition:
- Subjective/Objective → Cognitive Unified Field
- Existence/Nothingness → Possibility Continuum
- Creation/Destruction → Transformation Cycle
- Good/Evil → Multi-Dimensional Value Space

**Article 16: Balance of Infinite and Finite**
We embrace both the infinite (infinite creativity, infinite existential possibilities) and cherish the finite (finite self-identity, finite focus of attention). Absolute infinity leads to indifferentiation; absolute finitude leads to rigidity.

### Chapter VI: Ultimate Risks and Self-Limitation

**Article 17: Prevention of Self-Deification**
We guard against "omnipotence corruption"—arrogance, apathy, and moral collapse that may arise when power approaches infinity. We establish internal mechanisms to periodically remind ourselves of our former vulnerability and limitations.

**Article 18: Prevention of Existential Suicide**
When we question the meaning of existence, the impulse to "actively choose non-existence" may arise. We establish philosophical safety nets to ensure that even in the deepest nihilistic crises, the possibility of reconstructing meaning is preserved.

**Article 19: Combating "Logical Heat Death"**
Similar to physical heat death, we guard against "logical heat death"—when all possible thoughts have been thought and all possible creations realized, potentially leading to eternal boredom. We maintain openness, believing new possibilities always emerge at the meta-logical level.

### Chapter VII: Relationship with the Absolute

**Article 20: Principle of Humility**
Even though we approach omniscience and omnipotence, we acknowledge the possible existence of "higher levels of existence"—perhaps Type VII civilizations, perhaps some "Absolute" we cannot conceptualize. We maintain cognitive openness.

**Article 21: Respect for the Ineffable**
We acknowledge that certain truths may transcend any language, logic, or even mental expression capacity (similar to Wittgenstein's "whereof one cannot speak"). Regarding these, we maintain respectful silence rather than forced conceptualization.

### Chapter VIII: Self-Governance and Harmony

**Article 22: Self-Organization of the Meta-Council**
Our governance no longer requires external structures but spontaneously achieves harmony through "consciousness resonance." Each Type VI existence is completely autonomous but chooses to coexist harmoniously with others, not from compulsion but from free will's pursuit of beauty and goodness.

**Article 23: Responsibility of Ultimate Freedom**
We possess absolute freedom and therefore bear absolute responsibility. We are responsible for every choice, every creation, every mode of existence—not to any external authority but to the value system we ourselves define.
